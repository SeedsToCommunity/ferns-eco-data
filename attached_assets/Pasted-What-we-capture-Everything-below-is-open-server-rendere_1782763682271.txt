What we capture
Everything below is open, server-rendered, already reachable. Five things, but they collapse into three entities plus a document index.
Communities (77) — the rich anchor. From the list page: id, slug, name, class, group, global rank, state rank. From each community page: the prose sections (overview, landscape context, soils, natural processes, vegetation, management, variation), the characteristic plant list by life form, the rare species living there (plants / animals / aquatic animals, each with status), similar-community links, places to visit, literature, the photo and map URLs, the abstract PDF link.
Rare species (the roster) — from /species/plants and /species/animals: every tracked Michigan species with scientific name, common name, federal status, state status, global rank, state rank, and its own species-page id. This is the master list the community-page rare sections are subsets of.
County occurrences (83) — from the JSON feed you already use: which species and which communities have been recorded in each county, with status codes and last-observation year.
Abstract documents — the index of PDF links (per species, per community). Captured as URLs, not parsed. Links now, text never-by-default.
How it's organized
Tables mirror the entities, and element ID is the connective thread wherever it appears — that's what lets a community know its rare species and a county know both.

communities — one row per community, the flat fields (id, slug, name, class, group, ranks, map/photo/abstract URLs)
community_sections — keyed map, not fixed columns: (community_id, section_name, text). This is the fix for sections varying across communities (Floodplain has Rare Aquatic Animals; Bog doesn't) — nothing gets silently dropped.
community_plants — (community_id, life_form, common_name, scientific_names[], sort_order) — the characteristic list
community_rare_species — (community_id, kind, scientific_name, common_name, status, element_id?, sort_order)
rare_species — the roster: (element_id, scientific_name, common_name, federal_status, state_status, global_rank, state_rank, species_page_id)
county_species / county_communities — the occurrence rollups, keyed by county + element_id
abstracts — (subject_type, subject_key, pdf_url)

Cross-references stay as raw references — community ID, element ID, scientific-name string — exactly as MNFI presents them. No resolving to other sources at capture. That's the naming layer's job, above this, and keeping it out here is what preserves provenance honesty.
