the sources I pulled from during this turn, with what each contributed.
1. AnnArbor.com profile (June 7, 2011) — Rick Meader, "Getting to know your native plant suppliers: Greg Vaclavek, a native nurseryman"
URL: https://www.annarbor.com/home-garden/getting-to-know-your-native-plant-suppliers-greg-vaclavek-a-native-nurseryman/
The single richest source. Provided: Garden City upbringing, U-M School of Natural Resources bachelor's degree, post-graduation riverine ecosystem work in Huron and Manistee National Forests, the Tony Reznicek and Ed Voss specimen-identification connection, NAP work with Bill Schneider, 1998 nursery founding with Mike Appel, growth to ~160 species by 2011, Ann Arbor Wild Ones founding-era membership, his teaching philosophy and customer-pushback observations, his favorite plants (White vervain, White avens, Wild senna, Boneset), and credit for the Wild Senna photo collage as "Photos by Greg Vaclavek, Native Plant Nursery." Note: AnnArbor.com is archival; the live property has moved to MLive.
2. Nature and Nurture Seeds — "Meet Our Growers"
URL: https://natureandnurturseeds.com/pages/meet-our-growers
Confirmed his bachelor's in Natural Resource Ecology Management from U-M, his ecosystem field research and Ann Arbor Parks & Recreation Natural Area Preservation work history, and the philosophy quote: "Resting at the heart of the nursery is Greg's commitment to letting nature be the ultimate role model for growing native plants and working with landscape ecosystems."
3. Ann Arbor District Library event listing — "Gardening with Native Plants"
URL: https://aadl.org/node/4289
Confirmed Greg as a recurring AADL speaker on Michigan native plant species and the benefits of growing them. Also confirms the Farmer's Market Saturday presence from mid-April through September.
4. Ann Arbor District Library event listing — "Landscaping with Native Plants this Thursday"
URL: https://aadl.org/node/4286
A second AADL talk listing, separate evening event. Established the recurring-educator pattern rather than one-off appearance.
5. LocalWiki Ann Arbor entry — "Native Plant Nursery"
URL: https://localwiki.org/ann-arbor/Native_Plant_Nursery
Brief community-maintained page confirming the nursery details and pointing to the AnnArbor.com profile as the primary source.
6. Michigan commercial native plant/seed providers list (Wild Ones, River City Grand Rapids chapter)
URL: https://rivercitygrandrapids.wildones.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/21/2021/03/MI-Commercial-Native-PlantSeed-Providers-1.pdf
Listed Greg's nursery alongside Michigan Wildflower Farm, Ypsilanti Native Plant Nursery, Native Connections, Northern Natives, and Bill Schneider's Wildtype. Useful peer-context list of who's in this Michigan-native-seed ecosystem.
7. Going Native — Michigan native genotype nurseries directory
URL: https://www.waterfordmi.gov/DocumentCenter/View/588/Local-Resources-PDF
A separate, older directory entry showing the nursery as "Greg Vaclavek & Mike Appel" with the older PO Box 7841 address (the current site shows PO Box 2292). This is the source that confirmed Mike Appel as co-founder, and the address change suggests it predates a later move.
8. USDA Forest Service RNGR (Reforestation, Nurseries and Genetics Resources) directory
URL: https://rngr.net/marketplace/directory/thenativeplantnursery/view
A federal nursery directory listing — establishes that Greg's nursery was registered in the USFS/Southern Regional Extension Forestry nursery marketplace. Useful for credibility framing if you ever needed it.
9. Washtenaw County Rain Garden Contractor list (January 2026)
URL: https://content.civicplus.com/api/assets/11a78d36-ecb0-447e-a753-a15b3a5f5ff7?cache=1800
Listed Native Plant Nursery with Greg as contact, credited with building 3 rain gardens. Shows the nursery's listing was being maintained in regional contractor directories as recently as early 2026 — suggesting Greg or someone maintaining his information was still responsive at least into this year. Worth knowing.
