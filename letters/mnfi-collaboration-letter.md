**Re: Data Collaboration Request — FERNS Federated Ecological Resource Network System**

Dear MNFI Data Manager and Team,

I am writing on behalf of Seeds to Community, a Washtenaw County-based ecological restoration and community science organization. We are developing FERNS — the Federated Ecological Resource Network System — a federated data layer that aggregates authoritative ecological data sources to support restoration planning, community science education, and conservation research across Michigan and the Great Lakes region.

**About FERNS**

FERNS is built as an open, federated API that integrates multiple ecological data authorities into a single queryable system. Current integrated sources include GBIF (global occurrence data), iNaturalist (community science observations), Michigan Flora (Reznicek et al.), BONAP (North American plant distribution), Universal FQA (floristic quality assessments), the LCSCG prairie-fen species lists, NatureServe Explorer (global/national conservation status and ecological systems classification), and MNFI's own publicly available datasets — the 83-county element data tables and the natural community classification with abstracts.

All data is attributed clearly to its source and served through a provenance system that records the upstream origin, method, and retrieval date for every response. Where licensing requires it, we honor those terms explicitly.

**What We Have Already Built with MNFI's Public Data**

Using the county element tables available at mnfi.anr.msu.edu/resources/county-element-data and the natural community classification and abstracts from mnfi.anr.msu.edu/communities, we have integrated MNFI's public data into FERNS as a static source. This includes:

- All 83 county element records (species and community occurrences by county, with state and federal status where published)
- The full MNFI natural community classification: 77 community types with descriptions, ecological classes, landscape context, and characteristic species drawn from your published abstracts
- Community county distribution maps linked from MNFI's public pages

We are serving this data with attribution to MNFI and MSU Extension on every response, and we reference MNFI's terms of use throughout our data provenance layer.

**Our Collaboration Request**

We would like to discuss whether MNFI would be willing to share a richer occurrence dataset under a formal data agreement. Specifically, we are requesting access to **county-level or quad-level element occurrence records** — the kind of data that tells a restoration practitioner or researcher "this species or community type has been documented in this county or township during this time period." We have in mind records in the form of:

- Element name (species or natural community)
- County or general area (not precise coordinates)
- Observation date or date range
- Element global rank and state rank (as published)
- Quality rank or EO rank if available and appropriate to share

We are explicitly **not** requesting precise occurrence coordinates or site-level location data for rare, threatened, or endangered elements. We understand and respect MNFI's stewardship responsibilities under Michigan's Natural Resources and Environmental Protection Act and the data sensitivity policies that protect rare species from poaching and site disturbance. Our request is limited to the same coarse-grain spatial resolution already reflected in your published county element tables — with the addition of temporal range and quality context where those can be shared without sensitivity risk.

**Proposed Terms**

We are prepared to work within whatever framework MNFI requires. Options we would welcome include:

- A simple data use agreement or MOU governing the specific fields and resolution of shared data
- Attribution in all published materials and data API responses to MNFI and MSU Extension, consistent with your existing CC BY-NC 4.0 licensing
- Reciprocal data sharing: any community science occurrence data collected through Seeds to Community restoration and monitoring programs in Washtenaw County can be contributed back to MNFI's records
- Acknowledgment of MNFI as a data partner in any FERNS-derived publications or grant applications
- Co-authorship opportunity on any peer-reviewed publication specifically analyzing the FERNS dataset or its ecological findings

We would honor the spirit of your CC BY-NC 4.0 terms across any shared data — no commercial use, full attribution, and no redistribution of raw records in ways that would conflict with your licensing.
